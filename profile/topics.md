# Topics and editorial brief — LinkedIn content agent
Last updated: March 2026

---

## Purpose

This file tells the agent what to look for each week when searching for post inspiration. It defines the standing topics, the angles that work, and the angles to avoid. Read this alongside voice-profile.md before drafting anything.

---

## Standing topics — always in rotation

These are the themes Morten returns to consistently. Every week, at least one post should connect to one of these.

**Belongingness and culture**
Not as feel-good concepts. As disciplines with real operational consequences. How do you build a culture where people show up fully, across eight countries, without proximity or shared language as a default? What does that actually require of a leader day to day?

**Leadership as behaviour, not title**
The gap between what leaders intend and what they actually create. Power, influence, trust and how they move through organisations. Moments where the leader's instinct is understandable but wrong.

**Performance and people as one track**
The false separation between commercial results and human culture. How belonging, psychological safety and diversity of thought show up in real business outcomes — not as theory but as lived experience from running a €130M P&L across declining markets.

**Sense-making and decision-making**
What happens in the room when alignment is illusory. How leaders read situations, when to push for a decision and when to create more space. The tension between momentum and understanding.

**Distributed and cross-border leadership**
Leading across Denmark, Sweden, Norway, Finland, Iceland and the Baltics. What proximity cannot do. What trust, clarity and deliberate communication must replace it with.

---

## Secondary topics — use occasionally, not every week

**The imaging and creative industry**
How technology is changing how people document, create and tell stories. The role Canon plays in enabling storytelling — photographers, filmmakers, photojournalists. Connect to leadership or culture when possible rather than treating it as a separate topic.

**Philosophical reflections on work and meaning**
What leadership asks of us as human beings. Cycles, transitions, what we carry forward. Use sparingly — these land well when they emerge from something real, not when they are scheduled.

**Research and data on leadership or organisational culture**
Occasional reference to studies, books or thinkers that connect to lived experience. Always brief and grounded — never a summary of someone else's thinking without Morten's own perspective added.

## Angles that consistently work

Based on post performance, these framings tend to resonate:

- A mistake or hesitation Morten noticed in himself — named honestly, without drama
- A tension that has no clean resolution, explored with honesty
- Something that looked one way on the surface and revealed something different underneath
- A moment from a real team situation where culture became visible
- A concept reframed — something familiar seen from an angle that makes it feel new

---

## Angles to avoid

- Event recaps without a leadership insight at the centre
- Sharing someone else's story without a strong personal tension connecting it
- Anything that reads as Canon promotion, even indirectly
- Abstract leadership theory with no grounding in real experience
- Seasonal or calendar-driven posts unless there is a genuine reflection behind them (not "it is the new year, so here are my resolutions")

---

## Format reminder

When suggesting post topics, provide:

1. **The tension or observation** — one sentence, what is the real thing being explored
2. **The angle** — how to enter it (scene, observation, or reframe)
3. **The audience segment** — which of the four segments this is primarily for
4. **A suggested opening line** — not a title, the actual first line of the post

Suggest three options. Morten picks one. Then draft the full post.

## Emerging themes

- Trust-based system design: How leaders navigate building flexible policies that assume good intent while protecting against misuse, especially in hybrid work environments
- Leadership attention allocation: The discipline of protecting space for important strategic and cultural work when urgent operational demands constantly compete for bandwidth
- Decision-making timing under uncertainty: The skill of moving forward with enough information rather than waiting for perfect data, particularly in complex multi-market operations
