## Purpose

This file tells the agent where to look for relevant research, trends and thinking when generating post angles. It defines trusted publishers, trusted voices, and how to search. The goal is not to summarise reports — it is to find connections between current research and Morten's lived leadership experience.

## Search logic

When searching for content to inform post angles, follow these principles:

**Recency**
Prioritise research and reports published in the last 6 months. If nothing recent is available on a specific topic, go back up to 18 months. Always note when a source is older than 6 months.

**Relevance filter**
Only use research that connects meaningfully to one of Morten's standing topics — belongingness, distributed leadership, psychological safety, performance culture, sense-making, or the human side of strategy. Do not force a connection.

**Nordic and Baltic lens**
Where possible, find research with European or Nordic relevance. Global findings are useful but should be contextualised — what does this mean for leaders operating across eight markets with different cultures and languages?

**Primary over secondary**
Prefer original research reports over commentary or summaries. HBR articles interpreting a Gallup study are less valuable than the Gallup study itself.

**Connection over citation**
The goal is not to reference a report in the post. The goal is for the research to sharpen the angle — to give Morten a specific data point, a tension, or a finding that makes his lived experience more universal. The post should still sound like him, not like a research summary.

## How to use this file

When generating post angles on a scheduled run:

1. Search for recent content from the trusted publishers above on topics relevant to Morten's standing themes
2. Identify one or two findings that connect to something he could have experienced directly
3. Use the finding to sharpen the angle — not as the main point, but as the context that makes the personal observation more resonant
4. Note the source briefly in the topic suggestion so Morten can read the original if he wants to

Example of good use: *"Deloitte's 2025 Human Capital Trends report found that 63% of leaders feel unprepared for leading across hybrid structures. This connects to your belongingness theme — angle: what proximity cannot replace and what deliberate attention must build instead."*

Example of bad use: *"Here is a summary of the Deloitte report findings."*
